About

This game is just a very basic demo of our idea.

Physics games are fun and beneficial, but the problem is that most (if not all) rely on approximating the result in order to solve the game. For example, in Angry Birds players try to approximate the best angle and power to throw the bird with. In addition, during playing Angry Birds, players doesn't know anything about the physics concepts they apply when approximating the angle and power of the throw.

However, explaining the physics concept with the help of angry birds will be easier, since players already have thought and tried to approximate the solution of the game.

So why not combining both ? what if physics games were like any other games that is played because it is fun and challenging, but part of the game would be about explaining the concept and how we can calculate a near optimal solution and also provide data for calculating this solution and trying it in the game.

Here we illustrate a simple physics problem through a game where there flying objects with velocity, we need to fire a rocket with force F at a certain time to hit a specific flying object.

There are 3 levels, although 1 level is enough with 1 object to explain, they are made just for the sake of fun in the game.

Game Instructions
  • - Your task is to powerup the rocket with a force you decide at the correct time to hit the lightblue circle target without hitting the red targets
  • - You can see the rocket engine force at the bottom right corner
  • - Up/down arrow keys increase/decrease the decided force by 1N
  • - Press with Ctrl to increase/decrease decimal by 0.1N
  • - Press space to powerup the rocket engine with the force chosen
